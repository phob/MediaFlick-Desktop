Client settings

Player backend

Which player MediaFlick hands playback off to.

mpv handoff

mpv is controlled over a JSON IPC pipe — MediaFlick sends commands and confirms effects via observed-property events. This is the current default and supports chapter markers and segment skipping.

MPC-HC handoff

How MediaFlick talks to MPC-HC.

Slave mode (WM_COPYDATA) Launched once with /slave <hwnd>, then driven entirely over WM_COPYDATA. Bidirectional — pushes state/position notifications back, like mpv's IPC.

Slave mode requires a hidden message-pump window on the MediaFlick side to send and receive WM_COPYDATA; command IDs come from MPC-HC's MpcApi.h. Chapter markers, external subtitle URLs, and the mark-watched hotkey aren't available on MPC-HC and degrade gracefully.

Segment skipping

On MPC-HC, position is polled (~1 s) rather than pushed, so segment prompts may fire slightly later than on mpv. The skip behaviour itself is unaffected.
